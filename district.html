"Kerala Tourism: A Unique Journey into Paradise"

Nestled in the lush embrace of India's southwestern coast lies Kerala, a destination renowned for its exquisite natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique experiences that set it apart from all other tourist destinations. In this article, we'll embark on a journey to explore what makes Kerala tourism truly unique.

THRISSUR

Thrissur district was formed on 1 July 1949, with the headquarters at Thrissur City. Thrissur is known as the cultural capital of Kerala, and the land of Poorams. The district is known for its ancient temples, churches, and mosques.

ALLEPPY

Known as the Venice of the east, Alappuzha is a waterlogged district endowed with immense natural beauty and has emerged as a major tourist destination of the country. Today Alappuzha is well known for its beaches and backwaters.

ERNAKULAM

Recognised as the commercial capital, it is the most cosmopolitan city of Kerala. With the Chinese, Portuguese and British influences, Ernakulam has always been a land of varied cultural demographic. Ernakulam sees a large number of tourist arrivals every year.

WAYANAD

Known as the spice garden of the south, the land of paddy fields, and the home of the monsoon, Wayanad is a heaven like none other. Southernmost tip of the Deccan plateau, Wayanad is a land of virgin forests and misty peaks.
